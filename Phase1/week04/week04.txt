1. What is a dictionary in Python and how is it different from a list? Use a concrete example from this week's exercise to explain.

Unlike lists, dictionaries contain two elements; a key and a value. A value is conected to a specific key, they come in pairs. That means we can call a value tied for a specific key. In this excercise, it allowed us to connect informations about our patients (values) to the types of information it represented (keys). In other words, keys were like headers of a data spreadsheet, while values were the data.
